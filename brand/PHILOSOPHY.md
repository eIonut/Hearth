# Warm System

**Philosophy:** The machine is cold; the place you work from is not. A single flame, meticulously drawn, where the base burns the blue of a running process and the tip burns the amber of a kept fire — one gesture holding both the terminal and the home.

## Form & space
Everything begins from the rounded tile — the same soft-cornered rectangle the interface already speaks in, so the mark feels native to the surface it sits on. Inside that quiet field, a lone flame stands centered and unhurried, given generous dark space on every side. Nothing crowds it. The emptiness is not absence but hearthstone: the dark ground that lets a small fire read as warmth rather than decoration. Negative space is composed with the same care as the mark itself.

## Color & material
A restrained, deliberate palette drawn entirely from the working environment: near-black graphite grounds (#0d1117, #161b22), a process-blue (#2f81f7) at the flame's root, an ember-amber (#d29922) at its crown, with the transition between them calibrated painstakingly so the eye reads a single continuous heat rather than two stacked colors. A soft outer glow, barely there, suggests the flame throws light onto the tile around it. No gratuitous hue enters the system; every value earns its place.

## Scale & rhythm
The mark must survive the cruelest test — sixteen pixels in a browser tab — and still hold its identity, then bloom to fill a banner without a single edge betraying the enlargement. This demands geometry resolved to master-level precision: curves that are truly tangent, joins that are truly smooth, a silhouette that stays legible when everything else is stripped away. The wordmark, when present, sits beside the flame in a thin, confident sans, lowercase and calm, never competing with the fire.

## Composition & hierarchy
One idea, expressed once, expressed perfectly. The flame leads; the word follows; the ground holds them both. In the banner, the same flame anchors a wide, patient field where a single line of text does all the talking and the rest is breathing room — the composure of a workshop at first light, tools laid out, nothing yet begun. This is work meant to look labored over: the product of deep expertise, painstaking attention to every curve and every pixel, the quiet confidence of a mark that knows exactly what it is and shows nothing it doesn't need to.
